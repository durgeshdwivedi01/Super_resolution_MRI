CONCEPT LEGEND
Low-Resolution Input
Standard MRI acquisition — limited k-space coverage, large voxels, partial volume effects.
k-Space Extrapolation
SR extends k-space beyond the acquired region. High frequencies = fine detail = edges.
Multi-Frame / Shifted Acq.
Sub-pixel shifted acquisitions provide complementary frequency info for reconstruction.
Compressed Sensing SR
Sparsity in wavelet domain allows undersampled k-space → SR via L1 minimisation.
Deep Learning SR
CNN learns LR→HR mapping from paired training data. SSIM/PSNR supervised or GAN-based.
Hallucination Risk
DL SR can generate plausible-looking but anatomically incorrect fine detail — critical concern in clinical imaging.
Slice Profile / Through-plane
Through-plane SR crucial for isotropic voxels from anisotropic (thick-slice) acquisitions.
SR METHOD COMPARISON
CLASSICAL
Multi-frame registration + iterative reconstruction
✓ No hallucination
✗ Slow, motion-sensitive
k-SPACE EXT.
POCS, ALOHA, k-space extrapolation
✓ Physics-based
✗ Limited SR factor
COMPRESSED S.
L1 min, Total Variation, wavelet sparsity
✓ Undersampling OK
✗ Long recon time
DEEP LEARNING
CNN/GAN/Transformer SR (ESRGAN, SynthSR)
✓ Fast, high SR factor
✗ Hallucination risk
